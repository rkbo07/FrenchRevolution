August 26, 1789-

Declaration of the Rights of Man & Citizen


The Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen was a document that held the principles of liberty, equality, and fraternity. It proclaimed the inherent rights (liberty, property, security, and resistance to oppression) of all citizens. It was inspired by Enlightenment ideals, as it aimed to dismantle the arbitrary privileges of the aristocracy and clergy and establish a society based on the rule of law and individual freedoms. It served as a foundation for the subsequent development of constitutional and frameworks in France. It also inspired movements for liberty and rights across Europe and the world. Not only did it inspire others, it was inspired by the United State's Declaration of Independence.
It faced challenges with the government often failing to fully uphold its principles. However, it was a significant document in history.
