June 17, 1789-

National Assembly


The 3rd estate decided that they have had enough of this inequality, so they left the Estates General and declared themselves as the true representative body of France, calling themselves the National Assembly. They granted themselves exempt from all taxes. However, at the next Estates General meeting, they were barred from getting in.
The National Assembly drafted the Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen, giving them unalienable rights and freedoms and the principle of equality. They undertook the abolition of feudal privileges, the redistribution of church lands, and the implementation of secular reforms, like the Civil Constitution of the Clergy. The Civil Constitution of the Clergy was to reduce the number of bishops, which caused the immediate subordination of the Catholic Church and the authority of the Pope in France to the French government. It aimed to bring the clergy under state control by requiring them to swear an oath of loyalty to the Constitution.
The National Assembly did face a lot of challenges throughout the revolution. They had internal divisions between moderate and radical factions, threats from monarchist outside powers, and social unrest of the people. The Assembly dissolved with the onset of the legislative Assembly, marking a transition in the Revolution's path and paving the way for further revolutionary developments.
