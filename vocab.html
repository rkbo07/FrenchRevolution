Vocabulary


King

Louis XVI was king of France during this time. Although it was during the time of absolute monarchs, Louis XVI was easily manipulated by the noble judges of parement in Paris. The nobles and clergy asserted their power over the king and weakened the monarch.

First Estate

Clergy- church officials

Second Estate

Nobility- land owners

Third Estate

Everyone Else- peasants, merchants, commoners, etc.

Jacobins

Political group that emerged during the French Revolution. They were known for their radical ideas and actions - advocating for the abolition of monarchy and establishing a republic.

San-Culottes

Those who could not afford the fashionable breeches of the day and were "without pants". They were the poor working class of Paris. They used mob violence and intimidation to bring about political change.

Maximillien Robespierre

French lawyer and statesman. His vision was centered on forging a unified and indivisible France, established equality under the law and eradicating privileges. He spearheaded the Reign of Terror, using the guillotine for executions, even his own.

Marquis de Lafayette

He helped write the Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen.

Jean-Jacques Rousseau

He was a philosopher, writer, and composer. His political philosophy influenced the French Revolution and the development of modern political, economic, and educational thought.

Marie Antionette

Queen of France. She was born an archduchess of Austria and was the penultimate child and youngest daughter of Maria Theresa.

Napoleon Bonaparte

French emperor and military commander who rose to prominence during the French REvolution and led successful campaigns during the French Revolution. Which then allowed him to gain power, and he earned the title of First Consul. He later on expanded his empire, taking over the majority of Europe.

Olympe de Gouges

French playwright and political activist. She wrote the Declaration of the Rights of Woman and of Female Citizens.

Jacques Necker

Finance minister for Louis XVI.

Thomas Paine

Wrote political pamphlets and newspaper articles. Wrote Rights of Man.
