November 9, 1799

The Coup d'état of 18 Brumaire


The coup d'etat that overthrew the Directory and established the French Consulate with Napoleon Bonaparte at the helm, also marked the end of the French Revolution. Led by Abbe Sieyes, Talleyrand, and Ducos, they tricked the Council of Ancients into believing a “Jacobin plot” was occurring and led them away from Paris. The next day, Bonaparte fumbled a speech to the Councils of Ancients and 500 who refused to comply with their wishes and began to suspect the real plot. Sieyes and Lucien Bonaparte were able to fix the situation and coerce the Ancients to declare the end of the Directory and formation of a new consular government.
