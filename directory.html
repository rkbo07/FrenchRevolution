The Directory


The French Revolutionary government established after the disbandment of the Committee of Public Safety, included a bicameral legislature composed of the Council of 500 (proposed legislation), Council of Ancients (accepted or vetoed legislation), and 5 executive directors. They were able to elect government ambassadors, military officials, and other positions. However they were very weak with no real finances to fund their programs or enforce their will, laying the groundwork for the beginning of the Napoleonic Era. The Directory faced numerous challenges, including economic instability, political corruption, and internal dissent. Despite efforts to maintain stability, the government struggled to address these issues effectively, leading to periodic outbreaks of violence and social unrest. The Directory's reliance on military force to suppress opposition further undermined its legitimacy and contributed to its eventual downfall.
